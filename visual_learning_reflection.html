My Zimbabwe Origins

I'm from Zimbabwe - the only team member here who's a citizen from a non-first-world country.

Growing up in Zimbabwe in the 90s, 2000s, and 2010s taught me something fundamental:

Skills meant survival and success, not grades or good school names.

When infrastructure fails regularly, when you must collaborate to solve problems no textbook covers, when innovation means the difference between thriving and merely surviving - you develop a different understanding of what intelligence really looks like.

Capability Over Credentials

In Zimbabwe, I learned that real-world success comes from skills like adapting, collaborating, and solving unexpected problems...

Education's True Origins

This perspective made me curious about education's origins. Originally, learning was about preparing people for the real world...

Masters assessed apprentices based on actual capability, not isolated recall. Learning was about developing skills that mattered in practice.

So when did we decide that timed, isolated recall was a better measure of capability than actual performance?
